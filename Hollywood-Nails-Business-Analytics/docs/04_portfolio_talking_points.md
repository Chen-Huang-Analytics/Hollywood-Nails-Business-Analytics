# Portfolio Talking Points

## 30-second project explanation
I built a PostgreSQL business analytics project based on the operating model of a service-based salon. I designed the relational database, organized synthetic appointment and transaction data, created reusable SQL views, and wrote business queries covering revenue, customer retention, peak demand, service profitability, employee productivity, purchasing, and inventory control.

## What this project demonstrates
- Relational database design
- SQL joins and aggregations
- Window functions
- Common table expressions
- Business KPI development
- Customer retention analysis
- Operations and inventory analysis
- Translating business questions into analytical queries

## Interview example: finding a business problem
One useful analysis is the inactive-customer query. It identifies customers who have not returned in at least 90 days and ranks them by historical lifetime value. This gives the business a practical re-engagement list for CRM or email campaigns.

## Interview example: operational improvement
The peak weekday and peak-hour queries help compare demand with staffing. The results can support scheduling decisions, reduce understaffing during busy periods, and avoid overstaffing during slower periods.

## Interview example: inventory control
The inventory variance query compares expected and physical stock. Large repeated variances may indicate waste, recording problems, overuse, or process inconsistency.
